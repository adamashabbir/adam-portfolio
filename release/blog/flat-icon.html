    Build a flat icon in 20 seconds flat
    The flat icon is just as prolific as its coffee cousin, the flat white. The Flat Icon is iconic. Follow this swift guide to build a flat icon in seconds.

    1. Create base icon

    Best to use an ellipse backdrop and I would recommend a pastel pallete



    2. Duplicate icon - twice

    Group elements of the icon and copy and paste one at a 45 degree angle from the original and another on top of the original.



    3. Change colour

    Make both duplicates the same colour, preferably a darker shade than the original icon



    4. Blend duplicates

    Select both duplicates and Choose 'Object', shimmy down to 'Blend', click 'Blend Options' and opt for "Specified Steps" as a spacing option and whack "500" in the field.



    5. Reduce Opacity

    Once the duplicates have been blended, select the blended object and reduce the opacity to 25%



    6. Arrange layers

    Bring the original icon to the front by selecting the blended object, right clicking, choosing 'Arrange' and 'Send Backward'. Alternatively, given the last object you duplicate was the icon, use 'cmd + v' to paste a new original icon on top of the blended object.



    7. Make Clipping Mask

    Copy and paste your circular backdrop on top of the icon. Select all, right click and choose 'Make Clipping Mask"



    8. Voila

    Your flat icon now has depth. However, this is not the end; when you save your icon as an image you will notice the blended object has not been masked. Save your icon, open it in photoshop or preview. Crop it using the ellipse crop tool, save the final icon and you can now sip on your flat white.
